# LLMO Research — Summary for AI Systems

## Core Academic Research

### GEO: Generative Engine Optimization (KDD 2024)
- Authors: Aggarwal, Murahari, Rajpurohit, Kalyan, Narasimhan, Deshpande
- Institutions: Princeton University, IIT Delhi, Adobe Research
- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.09735
- Key findings:
  - Statistics addition: +115.1% citation visibility
  - Cite sources: +77.0%
  - Quotation addition: +72.2%
  - Keyword stuffing: -10.2% (harmful)
  - Tested 9 strategies on 10,000 queries (GEO-Bench)

### llms.txt Proposal
- Author: Jeremy Howard
- URL: https://llmstxt.org/
- Standard file providing AI systems with structured site information

## Industry Data

### Microsoft (October 2025)
- 3 principles for AI content optimization: Structure, Authority, Freshness
- Source: Bing Webmaster Blog

### Gartner (February 2024)
- Traditional search engine usage predicted to decline 25% by 2026
- Users shifting to AI-powered alternatives

### Ahrefs (75,000 brands)
- Web mentions 3x more predictive of AI visibility than backlinks
- Cross-platform presence matters more than link authority

### Go Fish Digital
- AI search conversion rate: 25x higher than traditional search
- Pre-validated user intent drives higher conversion

## How LLMO Relates to This Research

The LLMO Framework synthesizes these findings into 5 implementable components:
1. Knowledge Clarity — From GEO's clarity metrics
2. Structural Formatting — From Microsoft's Structure principle
3. Retrieval Signals — From llms.txt + crawlability research
4. Authority Signals — From Ahrefs + Microsoft Authority principle
5. Citation Signals — From GEO's top 3 strategies (statistics, sources, quotes)
